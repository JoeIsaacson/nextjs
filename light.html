Part 1 of 4

UXR across crypto payments

Working with a dedicated researcher and data scientist, we were able to dig deeper into the regular JTBD of sending / receiving crypto as a crypto trader.

What we learned

Baseline

Unfamiliarity with crypto payments

68% of Coinbase users were interested in paying with crypto. Only 4% ever reported successfully doing so.

Barriers

Lack of clarity and certainty

Lack of exposure, loss of funds, impact on gains and taxes were all flagged as potential barriers in terms of adoption.

Motivators

Various reasons to pay with crypto

Crypto rewards for spending were becoming common in the industry. Otherwise, the only other reason to pay with crypto was limitations imposed by the seller.

Part 2 of 4

Define strategic levers

A design sprint solely focused on the collection of issues across XFN teams. From here, we broke down the HMWs on how we can get people ready to spend, enable them to spend and lastly reward them to spend.

Strategic levers

Lever 1

Getting ready to spend

People perceived Coinbase as a place to buy, sell and trade crypto. But don't think of storing their spending money based on our lack of payment rails like Direct Deposit and easy saving and spending tools.

Lever 2

Spending anywhere

People would often buy crypto earlier in cycles and sell it in order to pay for an unforseen cost. With 67% of people living paycheck to paycheck, and the median Coinbase account balance at $243, liquidity is a critical lever.

Lever 3

Rewarding users

The crypto space was crowded with aggressive rates. We wanted to break through by offering high rewards on the crypto of your choice, including BTC, ETH, USDC and 14+ other assets.

Lever 1

Getting ready to spend

Allow people to get paid in crypto using Direct Deposit. And take the first step to becoming their primary crypto account.

Outcome

Impact

The launch of Direct Deposit lead to 80k+ sign ups and 11k MTUs in the first month, with 100m+ of assets custodied into Coinbase. This lead to an incremental $26 per user per year in trading volume.

We saw employees from Paypal, Amazon, Doordash, Walmart, Uber, Department of Defense, Google, Microsoft and the Mayor of New York, Eric Adams sign up for Coinbase Direct Deposit.

Lever 2

Support spending anywhere

Introduce people to the many ways of paying with crypto, including the Coinbase Visa Card Coinbase Commerce.

Outcome

Impact

We launched Coinbase Card with 150k+ sign ups leading to 1m+ MTUs. Users eventually made over 1m transactions, increasing the overal ARPU (average revenue per user) to become the most popular payment method for Coinbase outside of P2P. When compared to other cards, it consistently received the highest ratings because of it's rewards reate and superior UX.

Coinbase Card is currently only available in the US but is slated to launch in 24+ countries abroad.

Lever 3

Reward users in valuable ways

Promote spending through rewards, the 3rd most selected reason for spending crypto over cash

Outcome

Impact

Over the course of a year, we established Direct Deposit, Coinbase Card and grew each product to 14k and 1m MTU total.

Both products have been key to diversify Coinbase's revenue stream away from trading revenue. Coinbase generates revenue from both Direct Deposit and Coinbase Card.

Experimental

Make payments social by default

Balance privacy concerns deeply embedded in crypto while exposing users to new social ways to pay.
